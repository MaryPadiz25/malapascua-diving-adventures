EXPLORE UNDERWATER

Top Dive Sites

Malapascua is world-renowned for its diving, from thresher sharks to vibrant coral reefs.

🐟

Kimud Shoal

Thresher Shark Encounter

Early morning dives for thresher shark sightings at their cleaning station, with occasional manta ray encounters.

⚓

Gato Island

Tunnel & Marine Reserve

Famous underwater tunnel dive with soft coral formations, white-tip reef sharks, sea snakes, and rich biodiversity.

📷

Lighthouse Reef

Night Dive & Mandarin Fish

Magical night dives to witness mandarin fish mating dance. Shallow depth makes it perfect for beginners and macro photography.

🚢

Doña Marilyn Wreck

Historical Wreck Dive

A typhoon wreck covered in corals and home to lionfish and scorpionfish. Depth ranges 18–32m for advanced divers.

🌊

Chocolate Island

Macro Paradise

A macro diver's dream with frogfish, pipefish, seahorses, and varied topography. Max depth 18m — great for all levels.

🦈

Monad Shoal

Big Pelagic Encounters

50% chance encounters with tiger sharks, manta rays, hammerheads, or whale sharks. For experienced divers only.

FAQ

Malapascua is world-famous for thresher sharks at Kimud Shoal, accessible only at our dive depth. You'll also experience the spectacular Gato Island tunnel, vibrant coral gardens, macro diving opportunities, and wreck dives all within short boat rides. The island itself is untouched paradise with genuine local hospitality, not mass tourism.

November through May is peak season with excellent visibility (20-30m), calm seas, and the best thresher shark sightings. December to February offers the most stable weather. June to October sees rougher seas but fewer tourists and excellent macro photography year-round.

For most dives, you'll need an Open Water certification minimum. Thresher shark dives at Kimud Shoal require Advanced Open Water. If you're not certified, no worries — I offer full SSI & PADI certification courses, and you can try diving with our Discover Scuba program first.

Absolutely! Our Discover Scuba program lets absolute beginners experience diving in controlled, shallow waters. No prerequisites needed — just swimming comfort and enthusiasm. Many of my first-time divers have gone on to get certified and return for advanced adventures.
